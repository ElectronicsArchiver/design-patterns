Plan your day

We're looking forward to welcoming you on campus at our Open Day. Find out what will happen on the day.

Our Open Days are popular, so make sure you've booked your place. Our next events take place on Saturday 19 September and Monday 21 September 2015. 

  • Book your place
  • Group bookings for teachers

What happens on the day?

Our Open Day is packed full of opportunities to find out about studying at York, from talks, tours and mini-lectures to visiting the information stands to chat with staff and current students.

Events start from 9.15am and finish at 4pm. If you arrive early you can grab a coffee and have a wander around campus.

The full programme will be available closer to the event.

What not to miss

  • Subject talks and mini-lectures: Meet with staff and students from your chosen department, ask questions about your course and get a taste of what it's like to study at York.
  • Accommodation: Explore our accommodation and ask questions about living on campus.
  • An introduction to the University: Our Vice-Chancellor will talk to you about our history, achievements, aspirations and values.
  • Developing your graduate employability: Our Careers team will tell you about how we can help you to land your dream job!
  • Study, teach or work abroad: Find out how you can expand your horizons with an international experience as part of your studies through our Centre for Global Programmes.
  • The student experience at York: The Students' Union will talk you through life outside the lecture theatre, from sports and societies to democracy.

Need help?

Our staff and student ambassadors will be on hand to give you directions and answer any questions you may have.

Refreshments

A variety of cafés will be open from 8am. Full details will be in your programme.

Getting around campus

Our campus is not as big as it looks and it’s easy to get around. You can use the handy maps in your programme to help you get around.

If you plan to study Computer Science, Law, Management or Theatre, Film and Television you should also visit the east side of campus (Heslington East) to view the brand new facilities.

Heslington East is also home to our fantastic York Sport Village and three colleges, Goodricke, Langwith and Constantine, where you can view student accommodation.

You can travel between Heslington East and Heslington West on foot (about 15 minutes) or you can travel around campus free of charge on the number 4 or number 44 public buses.
