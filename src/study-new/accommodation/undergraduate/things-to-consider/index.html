Things to consider

Factors affecting the price of your room

Catered or self-catered?

Whether your accommodation is catered or self-catered, you'll have access to a shared kitchen.

If your accommodation is self-catered, your shared kitchen is where you will make and eat all of your own meals. If you have a catered package, you will be served breakfast and an evening meal (Monday to Friday, during term time) in your college’s dining hall.

  • Catering

Ensuite or shared bathroom?

Just over half of our rooms have shared bathrooms. You'll have your own wash basin in your bedroom, and share bathroom facilities with other students in your flat, corridor or house. Shared bathrooms are only used by one student at a time, and they’re cleaned daily (Monday to Friday).

If you have an ensuite bedroom you'll have your own bathroom (containing a toilet, wash basin and shower) in your bedroom. Ensuite bathrooms are cleaned monthly.

Economy, Standard, Premium or Deluxe?

We have a range of options to suit you and your budget, from economy rooms in older blocks to brand new deluxe accommodation with spacious shared kitchens featuring sofas, a television and a TV Licence.

Our price bands can also reflect the age, condition and size of our accommodation, as well as things like how many students you will be sharing your kitchen and bathroom facilities with.

  • Rooms and prices

Let length

Most of our rooms are for a 40-week let length, which covers the entire academic year including the Easter and Christmas vacations. We also lets 51-week lets which are ideal if you're planning on staying in York over the summer.

For 2015/16 we have some 36-week lets available. If you take a 36-week let, you'll need to vacate your room and clear your belongings during the Easter vacation.

Other things to think about

Location/college

All of our accommodation is on our campus, which is quick and easy to get around on foot, by bike or using the free shuttle bus between Heslington East and West. As a guide, it takes around 20-25 minutes to walk from Market Square on Heslington West to our Heslington East colleges.

Our colleges aren't related to our departments - you can apply to live in any college, no matter what course you take. Teaching can take place anywhere on campus, so don't feel like you need to live right next to your department. You might also want to consider the location of things like the Library, shops and sports facilities when making your decision.

  • Campus map

Living Learning Communities

Some of our colleges are home to residential learning communities built around shared interests where students live and learn together:

  • Halifax College Leadership Houses
  • Derwent College Global Community

You can let us know if you're interested in joining a Living Learning Community when you apply for your accommodation.

Accommodation style

We have a few different styles of accommodation:

  • Corridor-style layout
    Rooms on a corridor, which may be the length of the building, with shared kitchens on the corridor. Often all floors can be accessed by all residents within the building, but you'll be the only student with a key to your bedroom
  • Flat-style layout
    Rooms grouped together on one floor, with shared kitchen facilities and an entrance door to the flat
  • House-style layout
    Rooms on more than one floor within a building, with shared kitchen facilities and one common external entrance door

We'll ask you to let us know which one you prefer when you fill in the 'Factors' part of the application form. If you don't feel strongly about any particular layout, just put this option at the bottom of your list!

Viewing flats

During your application, we'll ask if you are interested in living in a viewing flat. These are select ground floor flats which are the same standard as our non-viewing flats, but we sometimes show them to prospective students during Open Days and visits.

We can't guarantee that we will use your room but, if we do, we'll give you some warning so you can make sure it's reasonably clean and tidy, and you'll get a £5 gift voucher as a thank you.
