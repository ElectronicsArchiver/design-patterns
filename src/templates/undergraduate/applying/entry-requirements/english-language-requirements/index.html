English language requirements

If you're a non-native English speaking applicant you must provide evidence of your English language ability.

You need to show enough fluency in written and spoken English to the required level of the course you're applying to.

Exceptions

You do not need to provide evidence of your English language if any of the following conditions apply:

  • You're a UK National
  • You're a National from a majority English speaking country as specified by the UKVI
  • You've completed a degree either in the UK or in a majority English speaking country

For more information see:

  • UKVI Tier 4 Guidance from 6 April 2015 (PDF)

Accepted tests and qualifications

The following tests are accepted for entry into degree-level courses:

  • International English Language Testing System (IELTS) (Academic)
  • Cambridge Certificate in Advanced English (CAE)
  • Cambridge Proficiency (CPE)
  • Pearson Test of English (PTE) (Academic)

The requirements for these tests vary by academic department.

You must have completed the test within two years of the starting date of your course. You cannot combine scores from more than one sitting of the test.

  • GCSE English Language: Pass GCSE English Language with Grade C
  • O Level English Language: Pass O Level English Language with Grade C

We can also accept the following school leaving certificates as proof of English language ability, if you are taking an English language subject and you meet the minimum grade required.

  • International Baccalaureate: Pass English A or obtain 5 in English B at either Standard or Higher Level.
  • European Baccalaureate: Obtain 65% in English.

If you've not met the departmental requirements

If you've taken one of the accepted tests but not met the departmental requirement, you may be eligible for one of our pre-sessional language courses.

These courses will provide you with the level of English needed to meet the conditions of your offer. You can apply online once you receive and accept your offer. Apply early to avoid disappointment.

  • Pre-sessional language course

If you are taking a pre-sessional English course and require a Tier 4 visa the following conditions apply:

  • Tests taken on or before 5 April 2015: If you meet the English language requirements as specified in your offer letter, we will accept these results until 5 November 2015. You should provide us with your test certificates as evidence of meeting your condition.
  • Tests taken after 5 April 2015: You must take the IELTS test at a UKVI approved centre. No other test can be accepted.

For more information about visas and English language requirements see:

  • Tier 4 (General) student visas (gov.uk)
