Help with living costs 2015 entry

Every UK student is entitled to government support for living costs (providing you're a UK national or have resided in the UK for three years, other than for the purposes of full-time education, before begin your universities studies).

The amount you're entitled to depends on your household income.

Maintenance loan (repayable)

You can apply for a maintenance loan (full-time students only) to help with living expenses. The loan is paid into your bank account at the start of term. You could be eligible for:

  • up to £4,565 if you live with your parents
  • up to £5,740 if you're living away from home outside London

Along with tuition fee loans, you don't start paying it back until you're earning over £21,000.

* From 1 September 2016 the student finance package will not include maintenance grants. You’ll still be able to apply for grants if you’re eligible for certain benefits, disabled or need help with childcare costs.

Extra help

In addition to the maintenance loans and grants, you may qualify for extra help if any of the following apply to you:

  • You're on a low income
  • You have children or dependent adults
  • You have a disability
  • You're a medical or social work student
